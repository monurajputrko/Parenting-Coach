    ABOUT

    Dr. Usha Gupta

    I am Dr. Usha Gupta, a parenting coach and a former Gynecologist. I am also a certified Neuro-Linguistic Programming Coach and Life Coach.

    After a rewarding medical career of over 30 years, I am now dedicated to this next chapter — a warm and open space, crafted to make the world a happier place, one family at a time.

    My Own Journey as a Working Parent


    My transition from a medical practitioner to a parenting coach is driven by an unwavering commitment to nurturing the well-being of families.

    Through my own experiences of raising a vibrant 26 year old daughter along with providing steadfast support to countless patients and their families, I intimately understand the unique challenges of parenthood.

    Like every parent, I've faced my share of trials—navigating emotional outbursts of a toddler, combating feelings of loneliness, and tackling picky eating habits. I've steered through the rebellious, insecure, and low self-esteem teenage years with our daughter. Safety and security concerns with having to leave our child alone at home, while I and my husband were at work added another layer of complexity.

    Balancing demanding work hours in a bustling medical career alongside my husband's own hectic schedule, we confronted the realities of being a nuclear family without the traditional external support system.

    These challenges not only shaped my parenting approach but also fueled my commitment to supporting parents facing similar struggles.

    So, I bring to you my 30 years of experience working with families as a gynecologist, enhanced by Neuro-Linguistic Programming and of course, my first-hand experience with the trials and tribulations of raising a child, ALL of it distilled into personalized one-on-one mentoring sessions for you.

    As a coach and mentor, I lead parents through personalized sessions, guiding them in the parenting journey and assisting them in overcoming their specific challenges.

    I achieve this by providing them with the necessary information and essential soft skills, enabling them to understand their children's behavior and adjust accordingly.
